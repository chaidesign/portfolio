Optimization of Course Management 

Time:  2018.10
Theme:  Web Design
Team:  Individual work
Role:  UX/UI Designer

Context:
During my work period in 2018-2019, I was mainly responsible for an online education project called Aculearn Online, which is targeting to Thailand market. There are two sub-projects included:
1 A platform for tuition centres to sell and teach courses online (including student portal, tuition centre portal and management portal).
2 A multi-people real time interactive online classroom.

It's a "from 0 to 1" big project, so I played various roles in the developing time: product manager, project manager, researcher, UX/UI designer, coordinator......This was actually a really challenging thing for me who just graduated at that time, and I have gained a lot of extremely valuable practical experience. Here I would like to share one part of  the work, to show you what I have learnt from it. 

Problem:
In the tuition centre portal, one of the most major tasks is course management. As there are a lot of contents contained in the course, how to design an efficient and convenient using flow  is very important. I drafted a solution at first, but after testing with my collegues, I found it's not friendly to use. So I started to try to redesign on the basis of the first version.

1/ Problem Define


Scoping

This is the whole map of our project and what we're going to talk about is parts of the functions in teacher side:

Specific Requirements: 
Through previous competitor analysis and interviews with teachers, we determined the needs of teachers in course management, including creating new courses, managing published courses, and checking the time arrangement to teach the class on time.

Current Design Analysis & Problem Statement

Based on the basic requirements, let's go through the current design, there are 6 main pages including all the function needed:

Class arrangement page: Teacher can check class arrangement here, just for viewing

Public courses page: For searching, filtering and creating the new one.

Problem:
1) Information and function repetition: the contents of these two pages are overlaping but without any connection.
2) Against common sense: as calendar is a common design which usually can provide several interactions: dragging, moving...users would be confused if it's not editable.
3) Rethink the usage senario and design components: is it appropriate to use the cards design here?

Previous Next

Here are the current 4 steps for creating a new course:
1) Fill in basic information: Name, grade, subject, introduction, photos, teacher.
2) Set the syllabus: Give topic of each lesson, uploading the teaching material.
3) Set class time: Teacher may teach more than 1 class at different time but with the same course contents.
4) Fill in other information: Price, free videos, expiry date.

Problems:
1) Information repetition: The 1st and 4th step are both about same type of information filling, any reason to seperate them?
2) Sequence and layout:  Reconsider about the priority and the possible results.
3) Unclear information display: what's the relationship between "time" and "class"? which one should be emphsized?
4) About design components: Use drop-down menu to choose time is common but not effient.

Insights and design principles:

Clearer connections

Consider to recombine the relevant sections based on the usage scenario and function

Vicinity of relevance

Put elements of the same type together

Finer instructions

Give finer instructions to reduce confusion

Right components

Choose the right components for different using purposes

Stage 2/  Define


Ideation

I assumed some more specific usage scenarios and re-analyzed the relationship between the three basic requirements. I would find the  connections and differences between them as the basis of my later design.

Key insignts: 

(1) For creating new course:
- combine the "information" part together as one step, to simplify the procedure
- integrate the "set class time" part with the schedule part, as they are both about "time", and different class can be regarded as the different events type in the schedule

(2) For coures management:
- integrate and differentiate the "schedule" and the "course list" together, as they have similar functions but are used in different scenarioes

Concept Development

For the new creating course flow: reduce to 3 steps (information -> syllbus -> class and time)
